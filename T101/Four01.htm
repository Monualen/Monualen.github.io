Frequency Domain Analysis for Fourier Series

The analysis of circuits and systems in the «  frequency domain » finds extensive application in Communication theory.

Spectrum of a signal, harmonics, bandwidth of amplifiers, filters and transmission channels are concepts widely used.

Essential mathematical instruments for that analysis are the "Fourier Series" (for periodic signals) and the

 "Fourier Transform"  (for non periodic signals).

Using these transformations any signal can be represented as an infinite sum of sinusoidal waveforms of different frequency.
This allows to:

 

  •    carry out analysis on the signal,

 

  •    introduce the concept of spectrum,

 

  •    study linear systems in sinusoidal state.

In this lesson the Fourier Series will be examined, that is to say the transformation of periodic signals and their study in the frequency domain.

The mathematical expressions examined in the previous page point out that:

a periodic signal s(t) of frequency F can be thought as formed by the sum of a « dc  » component, plus a sinusoidal signal of frequency F (fundamental), plus an infinite series of sinusoidal waves whose frequency is a multiple of F (harmonics)

s(t) = dc component + fundamental (F) + harmonics (n·F)

frequencies and amplitudes of the components form the spectrum of the signal, which is a representation in the frequency domain equivalent to the representation s(t) in the time domain. 
The calculation of the amplitudes of the spectral components is known as spectral analysis (or Fourier Analysis) of the signal:
