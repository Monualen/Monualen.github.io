Generation of frequency modulation - Frequency modulator

There are two basic methods of generating Frequency Modulation:

Direct method : It uses an oscillator, where the reactance of one of the elements in its resonant circuit depends on the modulating signal. Such component may be either a FET or a Transistor (the Drain-Source or the Collector-Emitter capacitance) or a Varicap diode (whose capacitance depends on the biasing voltage), also called Varactor. The variable reactance modulator uses this method.
The carrier frequency is stabilized by employing circuits such as the AFC (Automatic Frequency Control) and the PLL (Phase Locked Loop).

Indirect method : In this case the FM is obtained through a Phase Modulation, previously integrating the modulating signal. The carrier is usually generated by a crystal oscillator, which is very stable. 
Typical example of this method is the Armstrong modulator.

 Frequency demodulators

Detection of frequency modulated signal can be obtained through several circuits, some still in use and others obsolete.

Travis discriminator : It is based on the behavior of a tuned circuit, where the output amplitude is proportional to the input frequency. The output signal is then detected through a diode.
Foster-Seeley discriminator : It is based on the behavior of a tuned circuit, where the output signal phase depends on the input frequency. The original and the phase shifted FM signals are added, and the resultant is then detected through a diode.

Previous discriminators are sensitive to amplitude variations of the FM signal. An « amplitude limiter » is required in order to minimize these variations.

The most used frequency discriminators are:

Ratio detector : It is similar to Foster-Seeley discriminator but is not sensitive to amplitude variations of the FM signal.
Its functioning will be examined later on.
Quadrature detector : It is widely used in Integrated Circuits and works as follows: the direct FM signal, and the same signal phase shifted by 90° are multiplied. The average value of the resultant signal will depend on the frequency deviation of the input FM signal.
PLL detector : It is one of the applications of Phase Locked Loop. It is less sensitive to noise than previous circuits. Its functioning is examined in the lesson dedicated to PLL.
